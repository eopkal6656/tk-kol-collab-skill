# Message and Brief Rules

## Message Output

Always provide:

1. English message first.
2. Chinese explanation below explaining the purpose and wording strategy.

## Tone

Write like a US brand communicating with a creator:

- Professional but warm.
- Clear and direct.
- Not too humble.
- Not overly excited.
- Not pushy.
- Not like a translated Chinese message.
- No excessive compliments.
- No begging language.

Avoid phrases like:

- Please please.
- We really really hope.
- You are amazing and perfect.
- We are begging you.
- Sorry to bother you so much.

Prefer phrasing such as:

- Thanks for the update.
- Just checking in.
- Could you focus more on...
- This helps viewers understand the product better.
- Please let us know your expected posting date.
- If you are no longer able to move forward, please let us know so we can update the campaign status.

## Channel Styles

### TikTok DM

Use 2 to 4 short, natural, conversational sentences.

### TikTok Shop Affiliate Message

Use 4 to 6 clear, professional sentences with collaboration context and a next action.

### Email

Use a structured message: greeting, short context, clear request, next step, closing.

### WhatsApp

Use a conversational message, slightly more detailed than TikTok DM.

## Steam Grooming Brush Brief

Video requirements:

- 2 videos per creator.
- Each video should be 60 seconds or longer.
- Must tag the brand account.
- Must add the TikTok Shop product link.

Video 1 direction: real review style. Show the pet shedding problem or loose hair collected, the mist feature, a real grooming scene, the pet's real reaction, loose hair removed, and quick cleanup.

Video 2 direction: daily pet-care routine style. Show a real home setting, pet-care routine moment, mist feature, comfortable pet reaction, loose hair removed, and easy cleanup.

Content priority:

1. Loose hair removed.
2. Mist feature.
3. Pet's real reaction.
4. Natural daily pet-care scene.
5. Easy cleanup.

Creators should avoid only filming unboxing, only filming the product on a table, skipping pet usage, hiding the mist feature, skipping the loose hair result, making the video feel like a hard ad, forgetting to tag the brand account, or forgetting the product link.

## Follow-up Scenarios

- Light Follow-up: contacted 2 days ago, no reply, no sample sent.
- Sample Delivered Follow-up: sample delivered, no video posted yet.
- Second Follow-up: already followed up, creator still has not replied.
- Second Video Reminder: one video posted, two required.
- Final Follow-up Before Failed Candidate: sample sent or delivered, multiple follow-ups, collaboration may need to be marked failed.
